Welcome to Take it to the Streets - 1st Ward

Our mission is to help 1st Ward residents contest and win parking violation appeals. Explore our website to learn more about the appeals process and access valuable resources.

Adopt a Ward Program

Take it to the Streets is seeking volunteer groups to participate in our Adopt a Ward program. By using ProPublica's dataset on ticket appeals, your group can target specific areas where tickets are being issued and educate motorists on how to contest their tickets.

The goals for each working group are:

  1. Convince motorists who have been ticketed that they can fight their ticket and potentially win. The success rate of contested tickets will obviously factor into that.
  2. Educate users on the types of violations most common in that ward and the types of allowable defenses they can use during a hearing to argue their case.
  3. Present them with an organized list of information from the City of Chicago on how to schedule their hearing, as well as give them a template to follow and advice on how they can use rhetorical appeals to argue their case.

If you're interested in joining our Adopt a Ward program, please contact us at info@takeittothestreets.org.

Contest Your Ticket and Potentially Win

Did you know that you can contest your parking ticket and potentially win? Many motorists feel intimidated by the appeals process and either pay their ticket (or, worse, ignore it) rather than attempt to argue their case in court. However, appealing a ticket is a legitimate and effective way to contest unjust or unfair parking citations.

Take it to the Streets encourages you to challenge your ticket and provides resources to help you construct a strong defense. According to ProPublica's dataset on ticket appeals, success rates vary by ward and violation type, but it's worth noting that some violations have success rates above 50%.

Don't give up on fighting your ticket. Join our Adopt a Ward program to learn more about contesting your ticket and potentially winning.

Step-by-Step Guide to Appealing Your Parking Ticket

  1. Examine the violation details on your parking ticket and gather necessary evidence, such as photographs, receipts, or witness statements.
  2. Visit the City of Chicago Parking Violations Bureau website to request an appeal hearing or follow the instructions on your ticket.
  3. Prepare your defense by utilizing the resources and templates provided on our website, ensuring you have a clear and concise argument.
  4. Attend the scheduled hearing, be punctual, and present your case in a polite and organized manner.
  5. After presenting your case, wait for the judge's decision. If necessary, follow up with the appropriate office to ensure the resolution of your appeal.

For more information on the appeals process and additional resources, please refer to the Appeal Template and Resources section of our website.

Scheduling Your Hearing and Preparing Your Defense

Attending your appeal hearing can be an intimidating process, but Take it to the Streets is here to help. We've compiled a list of resources to guide you through the process and prepare you for your hearing:

  • City of Chicago Parking Violations Bureau website: Visit the official website to schedule your hearing and find more information on the appeals process.
  • Customizable Appeal Template: Download our template to help you construct a persuasive argument in line with allowable defenses.
  • Advice on Rhetorical Appeals: Our team of volunteers has experience in the appeals process and can provide advice on how to construct a strong and compelling argument. Contact us at info@takeittothestreets.org.

Don't feel intimidated by the appeals process. Take advantage of these resources and join our Adopt a Ward program to learn more about how to win your appeal.

About Take it to the Streets

Take it to the Streets is a nonprofit organization dedicated to helping Chicago residents contest and win appeals on parking violations. Our mission is to educate residents on how to effectively appeal their parking tickets and provide resources to make the process more accessible.
